Planning Your Trip To Singapore

Attractions

Sentosa Island. It is an island of theme parks in Singapore. This place can be reached by commuter sky train, bus or taxi, or by taking the Cable Car with breathtaking view of the city. Once you are here there are many activities can be done. You can sunbathe and swim in its artificial white sand shores Palawan, Siloso and Tanjong beach. You can visit SEA aquarium and explore by walking along and under the realm of the sea aquarium with more than 100,000 sea species from various habitats. Located in the island is the famous Universal Studios Singapore, one of the 6 universal studios theme parks around the world. It features at least 28 rides featuring roller coasters Battlestar Galactica and The Revenge of the Mummy, Jurassic Park Rapids Adventure, The Transformer 3D Ride, just to name a few. In Sentosa Island, you can also enjoy taking the Skyline Luge and the Giant Swing. You can also come close and swim with dolphins in the Dolphin Islands.

Gardens by the Bay. It is Singapore’s strategy to change the nation from a garden city to a city in garden. It is a horticultural destination of architectural marvel where visitors can walkthrough several botanical conservatories, go in a flower dome and experience cloud forest in the city.

Singapore Zoo, Night Safari, River Wonders and Jurong Bird Park. These are Singapore’s haven for wondrous wildlife and must visit for animal lovers. Singapore Zoo is set in a rainforest environment is home to thousands of animals of hundreds of species of mammals, birds and reptiles. Night Safari is a sanctuary to nocturnal animals hence it is only open at night. River Wonders is river-themed wildlife park that covers over 10,000 amazing land and aquatic animals. Jurong Bird Park is an aviary located in the west side of Singapore is a tourist attraction for bird lovers. This bird park has a collection of thousands of types of birds including parrots, flamingos, penguins and eagles.

Orchard Road. For travellers who love shopping Orchard Road is the target destination. It is a 2.5 km long upscale shopping area with a great deal of international renowned department stores with high-end fashion stores, cinemas, electronic shops, fine dining restaurants and expensive coffee shops. Be it day or night the place is bustling with crowd strolling, socializing, dining or looking for glamorous products.

Singapore Flyer. If London has London Eye, Singapore has Singapore Flyer. It is basically a gigantic Ferris wheel that serves as an observation wheel located in the downtown district. It has 28 air-conditioned capsules, and each capsule can accommodate a maximum of 28 passengers. Once a capsule has reached the top, the passengers can get a breathtaking 360o view of the city.

All these attractions and a lot more unmentioned places are squeezed in a bustling major business hub in a land area of 728.6 km2 only.

Food & Beverages

Though once part of Malaysia and with Chinese as the major ethnicity, Singapore has brought up its own flavor of Malay and Chinese food. The recommended places to try the different Singapore street food are Lau Pa Sat (or “Old Market”) Hawker Center located in the center of the business district which opens only at night from 6pm to 3am the next morning, and the Makansutra Gluttons Bay located opposite the Marina Bay Sands Hotel. There is a wide variety of Singapore street food to try. Chilli Crab, listed one of the most delicious foods in the world by CNN published on 23rd Aug 2022 by Kate Springer in CNN Travel website (https://www.cnn.com/travel/article/asia-best-street-foods-cmd/index.html), is a stir-fried crab dish doused in sweet and spicy savoury tomato sauce and served with sweet buns. Dim Sum which is internationally known is a Hong Kong inspired variety of stuffed chicken, veggies, pork and seafood. Char Kway Teow is a stir-fried rice noodles made of rice noodles, dark sauce, fish, bean sprouts, eggs, sweet cause and Chinese sausages, and is considered a staple food for the locals. Bak Kuh Teh, meaning “Meat Bone Tea’ is a pork rib soup boiled in broth of herbs and spices mix with garlic and cinnamon but not to worry there is no tea mixed in the soup. Singapore also has its own version of barbeque which is called “Satay”. It can be chicken, beef, mutton or shrimp candied and skewered, then served with peanut sauce and white onions. However, the most memorable food for those who have stayed then left or just visited Singapore is the Hainanese Chicken Rice. This is the Singapore dish that everyone misses who left the country. It is special because of the spices and sauces used in steaming the chicken and on the rice served on the side which gives the dish its unique and so delicious taste. Aside from food Singapore also has drinks to try like the Singapore Sling, a mix of tropical fruits, pineapple juice and some alcoholic drinks. Ice Kachang a dessert made of crushed ice with red beans, grass jelly and sweet flavored syrup. It is a favourite among kids and adults too. Teh Tarik, the local brew of milk and tea is commonly drunk in the morning or every after meal. And the most highly recommended food when in Singapore – Durian! locally considered as the king of fruit. Banned in public transport, it’s just simply indescribable but definitely a must try for visitors.

Events

Singapore National Holiday held on the 9th of August every year commemorates the nation’s Independence Day. It is the most celebrated event of nation with the much-loved National Day Parade, public address by the Prime Minister, aerial acrobats and fireworks to close the celebration with a bang.

Setting aside the public holidays, the most much awaited event in this marvelous place is the Singapore Grand F1 Night Race which is held annually in the month of September. It is a 3-day event of Formula One night racing that takes place in the streets of Marina Bay Street Circuit. Spectators can watch from a premium grandstand where tickets cost from as low as S$38 up to S$1,788 as of year 2022.

Standard Chartered Marathon event is the most much awaited marathon event in Singapore and it is sponsored by Standard Chartered Bank. This event is held every first week of December Annually.
