Discovering Singapore

Brief History

Singapore locally known as Singapura and officially known as Republic of Singapore is a sovereign city-state located in Southeast Asia. The name ‘Singapore’ derives from the Sanskrit Singa Pura (‘City of the Lion’). It was established in the 13th century as a port settlement and served as a trading route between countries from the Indian Ocean and South China Sea. It became a British colony from the early 19th century to mid 20th century and was converted to a major trade hub that attracted immigrants from China, India and the Malay Archipelago. It gained its independence from Great Britain in 1946 and became part of Federation of Malaya which is now Malaysia. It separated from Malaysia and became a sovereign city-state on August 9, 1965, commemorated as Singapore National Day.

People & Language

Singapore has a population of almost 6 million and comprises of 3 major ethnicities: Chinese, Malay and Indian. Today, Singapore is a melting pot of different cultures. People from various parts of the globe have come to Singapore to do business or leisure, visit, work and some permanently live. English, Chinese Mandarin, Malay and Tamil are the official languages of the country. English is the most used language in the city. Though Malay is considered as the National Language, it is only the third most used. English language is the most widely used because it is the working language of businesses since it is considered as the global business language. In the streets of Singapore many speak Singlish short for Singapore English, a patois language called Singapore Colloquial English by academics. It is an English language spoken in a thick Chinese accent with some mix of Chinese words in it but still communicable to most variations of the English language. Mandarin is the second most used language since Chinese is the largest ethnic group making up almost ¾ of the country’s population. Malays are the original settlers of Singapore and are the second largest ethnic group. They speak the Malay language called Bahasa Melayu. The Indians are Singapore’s third largest ethnic group and mostly are Tamil speaking because most of them are from the Southern part of India after the British settled in Singapore in 1819.

Geography & Weather

Located in the Southeast Asian region specifically at the end of the Malayan Peninsula bordering Malaysia in the north, Indonesia in the west and south, and the South China sea in the east. Singapore is generally a flat land that has a land are of 728.6 km2, its highest peak is Bukit Timah at only 166 meters high. It is a tropical country located near the equator with abundant rainfall and high humidity throughout the year. The average daily maximum temperature is 31.4°C (88.5°F) and the average daily minimum temperature is 24.5°C (76.1°F) (source http://www.weather.gov.sg).

Top Universities

The National University of Singapore or NUS founded 1905 considered as Singapore’s flagship university is ranked 11th in the World and ranked 1st in Asia. This school is now ranked 7th in the world for engineering and technology subject area and ranked 6th for Civil Engineering. According to https://www.topuniversities.com/ for the year 2021.

Nanyang Technological University or NTU is one of the top universities in Singapore. It has recently broken into the top 40 universities in the world, the university rose 10 spots to rank 36th place globally as posted in its website https://www.ntu.edu.sg/. The university offers undergraduate and postgraduate program for students in engineering, business, science, humanities, arts, social sciences, education and medicine. It has a Smart Campus that serves as a testbed for tomorrow’s technologies.

Singapore Management University or SMU established year 2000 is a premier university in Asia. It offers programs for undergraduate, postgraduate research programs for College of Integrative Studies, School of Accountancy, Business, Economics, Computing and Information Systems, Law and Social Sciences. It claims to be internationally recognized for its world-class research and distinguish teaching according to their website https://www.smu.edu.sg/about.

Economy

Singapore is an economic powerhouse, it remains one of the strongest anchored with political stability, low corruption index and transparent institutions. It is the 5th most competitive country in the world for doing business, 4th largest recipient of Foreign Direct Investments FDI and 1st in the world for political and operational stability. This is according to the Singapore government website https://www.edb.gov.sg/ under Why Singapore link. After its independence, Singapore started as a low-income economy but after several decades it is now a high-income economy with a gross national income of US$54,530 per capita as of 2017. Manufacturing sector particularly electronics and precision engineering, and services sector particularly information and communications industries and finance and insurance industries, remain the strongest pillars of the country. This is according to the World Bank Organization, published in their website date last updated April 9, 2019. Despite the country has no natural resources, Singapore continues to be one of the strongest and most stable economy in the world.

Competitive Economy, Transparent Government, Easy Infrastructure, Strategic Location, Competent Labour Force, Efficient Tax System, Abundant Finance at Low Interest Rates and Easy Company Incorporation Process. These are factors make Singapore the world’s top country when it comes to doing business. (source: intuitconsultancy.com on the topic "Why Should You Consider Doing Business In Singapore?" published November 1, 2018)
